3‑D Printing in Manufacturing & Industry

Source 1

https://www.sciencedirect.com/science/article/pii/S2666412721000441 ScienceDirect This research article reviews how 3‑D printing technologies align with Industry 4.0, focusing on sustainability and material efficiency. It argues that additive manufacturing significantly reduces waste, energy consumption, and human involvement in traditional production. The paper describes how continuous improvements in material science and process automation make 3‑D printing more viable for industrial-scale fabrication. Companies can leverage these advancements to produce complex components with less post-processing and fewer defects. Furthermore, the study discusses the integration of robots and smart controls to improve productivity and reliability. Overall, it offers a forward-looking perspective on how additive manufacturing is transforming manufacturing paradigms toward a more sustainable and efficient future.

Source 2

https://ninjatek.com/learn/3d-printing-by-industry/industrial-manufacturing/ NinjaTek NinjaTek details how industrial manufacturers utilize 3-D printing to maintain production flow and reduce downtime. The article explains that additive manufacturing enables on-demand production of replacement parts, meaning companies no longer need to rely on long lead-time orders. With durable, flexible TPU filament, 3-D printing accommodates both rigid machine parts and protective components. This approach allows companies to quickly replace or retrofit parts without expensive inventory. The source also highlights how 3-D printed tools and fixturing can be tailored for specific machines and tasks, enhancing efficiency. It argues that industrial adoption of 3-D printing depends not just on the technology’s speed, but on its ability to solve real operational problems.
